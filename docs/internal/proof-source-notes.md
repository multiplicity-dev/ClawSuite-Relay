## Source: "We accidentally recapitulated a C-suite"

Primary local source artifact:

- `/home/dave/Documents/Notes/marketing/content-ideas/c-suite-recapitulation-case-study.md`

### What happened

During a real workstream, the president overrode the CEO agent's authority and told the organization
to stop responding to CEO-routed product/workshop execution tasks.

What followed was the important part:

- multiple specialist agents respected the override
- the later resume instruction was interpreted as scoped rather than blanket
- CFO independently recognized that a related request still belonged to the paused workstream and
  held for explicit clearance rather than proceeding automatically
- the organization corrected course without the human manually re-briefing every agent

### Why this is useful proof

This is one of the strongest demonstrations that Relay is not just message passing.

It shows:

- persistent organizational memory across agents
- authority and caution surviving across turns
- cross-agent context influencing later judgment
- behavior that looks closer to a real organization than to one-shot subagent spawning

### What it is best used for

- written proof artifact for the README or linked docs
- case-study source for the website
- launch-post material where the claim is "persistent inter-agent context changes behavior"

### Caution

The raw note is still a local content-idea artifact, not a public-ready case study. Public use
should tighten the sequence and remove internal framing that does not matter to outside readers.

## Source: CLO recovery through Legal Assistant orientation and QC

### What happened

A CLO session became context-bloated during a planning+integration turn.

Legal Assistant inspected the failing state, produced a compact re-orientation for a fresh CLO
session after reset, and the new CLO then resumed work and reused Legal Assistant as a standing
QC/planning lane.

### Why this is useful proof

This episode shows:

- recovery through a persistent specialist lane rather than a full manual re-brief
- visible handoff during recovery, not hidden orchestration
- a QC lane behaving like an ongoing organizational role rather than a one-shot helper
- continuity of specialist relationship surviving an interrupted session

### What it is best used for

- README proof excerpt about recovery and persistent QC lanes
- website proof paragraph about visible handoff after failure
- internal source for a future longer public case study

### Caution

The underlying failure itself was broader than Relay and should not be misframed as a Relay-native
bug. The proof value here is the recovery pattern and continued QC behavior, not the mere existence
of context-bloat.
